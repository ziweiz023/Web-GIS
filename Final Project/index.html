Understanding Climate Vulnerability of Delivery Drivers in NYC

Heat, Infrastructure, and Flood Risk

Our Climate Vulnerability Index (CVI) for New York City synthesizes three critical spatial layers—average annual surface temperature to capture urban heat exposure, infrastructure site density to reflect resource access, and coastal & storm‑flooding predictions to assess flood risk—into a single composite map. By combining and normalizing these factors at the neighborhood level, the CVI highlights the areas where environmental stresses and infrastructure gaps overlap, revealing where delivery workers and other outdoor laborers are most vulnerable. Use this interactive map to identify priority zones for targeted improvements—such as cooling centers, safe rest areas, and flood‐resilient facilities—to strengthen community resilience and equity across boroughs.
